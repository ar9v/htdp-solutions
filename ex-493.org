* Exercise 493

#+begin_quote
Argue that, in the terminology of intermezzo 5, =invert= consumes $O(n^2)$ time when the
given list consists of $n$ items.
#+end_quote

For each recursive call in =invert=, we must call =add-as-last=, which itself processes the
list. Roughly speaking, the sum of all the steps in a given call to =invert= is the sum
from 0 to $n - 1$ of what =add-as-last= takes, which is $n$. That gives an abstract time of
$\frac{1}{2} n(n - 1)$ (established in intermezzo 5), which is $O(n^2)$.
