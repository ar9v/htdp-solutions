* Exercise 519

#+begin_quote
Is it acceptable to impose the extra cost on =cons= for all programs to turn =length= into a
constant-time function?
#+end_quote

Maybe not wholesale, since, as the book states, there are a multitude of examples in which
we use =cons= without ever needing to compute the =length= of the result. However, it /is/
a useful structure to have, separately. Consider a program that is "read-heavy": if
we know beforehand that we'll take on the upfront cost of creating the structure once and
that the bulk of the program will consist of leveraging =length=, making =cons= more
expensive might be worth it.
