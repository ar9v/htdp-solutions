* Exercise 518

#+begin_quote
Argue that =our-cons= takes a constant amount of time to compute its result, regardless of
the size of its input.
#+end_quote

#+begin_src racket
  (define (our-cons f r)
    (cond [(empty? r) (make-cpair 1 f r)]
          [(cpair? r) (make-cpair (+ (cpair-count r) 1) f r)]
          [else (error "our-cons: expected a list, got " r)]))
#+end_src

In each branch, we call a single operation which doesn't require recurring and that we can
assume takes constant time: =make-cpair= for the first two branches and =error= for the last
branch. In the first branch, =make-cpair= simply specifies =1= as the count, which evaluates
to itself. In the second branch, it uses the =cpair-count= of =r=, its second argument. Since
that count is, however, just an accessor, its a constant time operation and thus the
whole call is constant time.
