#+begin_quote
You can also place the first queen in all squares of the top-most row, the right-most
column, and the bottom-most row. Explain why all of these solutions are just like the
three scenarios depicted in figure 173.
#+end_quote

All of those scenarios can be achieved by manipulating the scenarios presented in figure
173. For example, rotating the first board 90 degrees counter-clockwise places the queen
in the second column, in the first row. Flipping the second board on its horizontal
axis gets you the scenario where you've covered the bottom most spot of the right column.
And so on.

#+begin_quote
This leaves the central square. Is it possible to place even a second queen after you
place one on the central square of a 3 by 3 board?
#+end_quote

It is not. Once you place a queen on the central square, you threaten all remaining
squares.
