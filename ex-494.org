* Exercise 494

#+begin_quote
Does the insertion =sort>= function from chapter 11.3 need an accumulator? If so, why? If
not, why not?
#+end_quote

It can't really take advantage of an accumulator. Say we accumulate the list of sorted
items: if the items to be sorted are sorted in ascending order, =insert= will take on
the order of $n$ steps to add a candidate anyways.

(It /does/ help with space, though, since we the recursive call to =sort>= winds up being
a tail call, but that's another matter)
